Hypertension

Hypertension is condition when the blood pressure. This causes the heart to work more and pump extensively and if not controlled may result in increased risk of kidney diseases, heart failure, stroke. High risk factors are obesity, alcohol consumption, smoking, increased salt intake, stress, uncontrolled diabetes. Thus it is essential to keep these factors under check. Keep BP under control, regular monitoring. Include lifestyle changes and diet choices.

Infertility and ART

Struggling with Infertility? Fear not. Fertility struggles are no straightforward path, they’re messy and complicated and unpredictable.ART includes all fertility treatments in which either eggs or embryos are handled. It works by removing eggs from the ovaries. The eggs are then mixed with sperm to make embryos. The embryos are then put back in the parent's body. In vitro fertilization (IVF) is the most common and effective type of ART.

Diabetes

Diabetes is a highly on-the-rise non- communicable chronic condition that occurs when glucose in your blood is too high. Glucose is your body’s main source of energy. Whe the body is not able to utilize this gucose, it causes a rise in its levels leading to the condition. It is characterized by risk for damage to the kidneys, nerves, and heart. Diabetes can be treated and its complications avoided or delayed with diet, physical activity, regular screening and treatment for complications.

Geriatric Health

Look out for the health of the elderly in your family. Geriatric age group is prone to many health problems, and need curative, rehabilitative, psychological and social care. Common problems include fall, ulcers, dementia, urinary incontinence. Chronic problems include arthritis, cataract, impaired hearing and vision, cardiac problems, cancer, sleep disorder. It is essential to keep a vigilant eye on such issues and get a prompt treatment.

Stay Fit

Staying fit is not just a fad but a way of life. Prevention is the best cure, and staying fit is the way. Engaging in at least 30 minutes of moderate intensity exercises such as walking, swimming, cycling or jogging. No smoking. Cutting down alcohol consumption. Reducing salt, sugar intake. Increasing the intake of fresh fruits and veggies, high-fibre and whole-grain foods. Keeping a check on body weight. Monitoring blood glucose and BP. Adequate and quality sleep.
